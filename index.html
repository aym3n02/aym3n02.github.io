Experience

  • CIRAD
    Montpellier | MAI 2024 – SEPTEMBER 2024 | 4 Months

    This internship involved developing an artificial intelligence model capable of taking a satellite image as input and producing an output image of the same size, with a different color for each surface element, corresponding to the identification of the nature of that surface, known as a land cover map. To achieve this, we used deep learning methods to design an efficient prediction model.

    One of the challenges of this approach is being able to predict class values for an entire satellite image (34,868 columns x 43,732 rows = 1,524,847,376 pixels, encoded as unsigned integers UInt16). Due to the large size of satellite images, a neural network cannot process the entire image as input. We, therefore, had to divide the image into small windows on which predictions could be made. To obtain a prediction for the entire image, we then had to reconstruct the prediction from these small windows. This reconstruction can be done in several different ways, yielding more or less acceptable mosaic results, and we explored various reconstruction methods to determine the best one.

    The outcome of this internship was the production of an efficient model, deemed suitable for production by experts. Additionally, a program was developed that uses this model to label an entire satellite image. It is worth noting that this model achieved a high level of precision compared to remote sensing models, with certain types of trees being predicted with remarkable accuracy, in a way never before done in the literature.

    A scientific paper has been submitted for publication on this work, explaining the various challenges we encountered and how we were able to resolve them.

Projects

  • Pneumonia detection
    School project

    I developed multiple models aimed at detecting pneumonia from X-ray images, which can be a challenging task for doctors. The blur in X-ray images might be due to the illness or could simply be a natural characteristic of the image, making it hard to distinguish between the two. To tackle this, I compared various machine learning approaches, including the random forest method and a range of deep learning architectures, from a single perceptron to more complex Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). Through this work, I achieved a model with 95% accuracy.

  • Image Enhancement Techniques for Cancer Detection
    School project

    In cases where lung cancer is present, medical scanners often face difficulty capturing clear images due to the patient's breathing during the scan. The inhaling and exhaling cause the cancerous mass to move, resulting in a blurred image that complicates accurate measurement of its size and position.

    To address this, as a team of 3 engineering students, we developed complex mathematical methods to correct for this motion, allowing the cancer to appear more clearly in the final image. Our approach significantly improved precision, as confirmed by a medical professional, and we published a scientific article detailing this work.
